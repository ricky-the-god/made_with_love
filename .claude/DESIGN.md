# Made with Love — Design.md

## 1. Design Intent

**Made with Love** should feel like opening a cherished family memory box, not using a generic recipe app.

The design must communicate:
- warmth
- intimacy
- nostalgia
- safety
- cultural richness
- quiet elegance
- emotional comfort

The product is centered on a living family tree, personal recipe books, memory preservation, guided cooking, and optional cultural discovery. The design should always reinforce that recipes are connected to **people, stories, grief, tradition, and love**, not just ingredients and instructions.

### Base palette
Use a warm, muted, emotional palette.

#### Suggested neutrals
- warm cream
- parchment
- oat
- almond
- soft taupe
- faded cocoa
- light warm gray

#### Suggested accent colors
- cinnamon
- terracotta
- dusty rose
- sage
- olive
- muted plum
- soft amber

#### Suggested darks
- espresso
- warm charcoal
- deep brown

### Color behavior
- backgrounds should feel soft and breathable

- harsh pure black should be avoided when possible

- cream / parchment = memory, softness, readability

- dusty rose / plum = tenderness, nostalgia
- terracotta / amber = food, warmth, culture

---

## 6. Typography Direction

Typography should feel warm and literary, while remaining readable in a product UI.

### Typographic roles
Use a combination of:
- one elegant serif for emotional headings
- one readable sans-serif for body and UI text
- optional handwritten accent only in small decorative moments

### Tone
- headers should feel like a storybook or heirloom cookbook
- body text should remain highly readable
- decorative scripts should never hurt usability

### Recommended style pairing
- **Display / headings:** refined serif
- **Body / UI:** soft humanist sans-serif
- **Accent / quotes:** subtle handwritten or script-inspired style

### Rules
- avoid overly geometric cold typefaces
- avoid hyper-tech startup typography
- avoid using handwritten fonts for full paragraphs
- prioritize readability in cooking mode

---

## 7. Texture and Surfaces

Surfaces should feel tangible and warm.

### Surface ideas

## 8. Layout Principles

### Overall layout philosophy
The app should feel spacious, intentional, and calm.

### Rules
- favor generous spacing
- favor breathing room over density
- group content clearly
- let emotional content stand out
- avoid dashboard clutter

### Structure preference
Use layered hierarchy:
1. person
2. recipe
3. memory
4. interaction

The app is not just content-first.  
It is **relationship-first**.

## 10. Recipe Book Design

Every family member should have a cookbook that feels personal to them.

### Design principle
A cookbook is not just a list.  
It should feel like a reflection of the person.

### Personalization dimensions
Cookbooks should be customizable based on:

#### A. Age of the person
The cookbook style can subtly adapt depending on the family member’s age or generation.

Examples:
- **Grandparent / elder cookbook**
  - more classic
  - heritage-forward
  - deeper warmth
  - stronger paper / heirloom feel
  - more elegant typography
  - archival mood

- **Parent generation cookbook**
  - balanced warmth
  - practical and story-rich
  - family-centered
  - soft but structured

- **Young adult cookbook**
  - cleaner
  - more modern
  - lighter layout
  - more visual
  - easier remix and save patterns

- **Child cookbook**
  - playful but still tasteful
  - bigger visuals
  - simpler step cards
  - more approachable color accents
  - friendly cooking guide tone

#### B. Region / country / cultural context
Cookbooks should subtly adapt to the person’s region or recipe origins.

This should **not** become stereotypical or theme-park design.  
It should be respectful, gentle, and design-led.

Examples:
- layout motifs
- border styles
- illustration influences
- material tone
- color accents
- icon treatment
- recipe page ornamentation

### Cultural customization should be:
- subtle
- elegant
- opt-in or data-informed
- non-caricatural
- respectful of diversity within regions

### Example approach
A Vietnamese family cookbook and an Italian family cookbook should not become clichés.  
Instead, they may differ through:
- color temperature
- visual rhythm
- ornament density
- decorative motifs
- typography accents
- recipe card framing
- photo presentation style

- practical cooking utility
- emotional storytelling

### Page hierarchy
1. recipe title
2. linked person
3. cultural / country context
4. memory or story
5. ingredients and steps
6. notes and variations

### Visual feel

## 13. Guided Cooking Design

This is one of the most memorable parts of the product.

### Goal
The guided cooking mode should feel like:
- being lovingly taught
- being gently accompanied
- learning from family in a safe, comforting way

### Visual behavior
- large readable step cards
- simple ingredient reminders
- warm progress indicators
- soft motion transitions
- non-stressful pacing
- optional timers that feel integrated, not harsh

### Animated family guide
The guide should be:
- stylized
- cartoon-inspired
- warm
- expressive
- comforting
- respectful

### Important ethical rule
The guide must never feel like an eerie simulation of a deceased loved one.  
It should feel like a **memory-inspired companion**, not a digital resurrection.

## 14. Discover Design

The Discover experience should expand the world of the app while keeping the same warmth.

### Discover should feel like
- visiting another family’s table with permission
- discovering culture through recipes and stories
- respectful curiosity
- intimate exploration, not social-media chaos

### Content types
- public family trees
- public recipes
- country collections
- cultural story collections
- featured culinary figures

### Design rule
Discover must remain softer and more editorial than feed-like.

Avoid:
- noisy social cards
- engagement bait design
- infinite-scroll addiction patterns

Prefer:
- curation
- featured collections
- calm browsing
- story-led discovery

## 15. Favorites Design

### Two favorite types
- personal favorites

### Visual distinction
- personal favorite = subtle, individual save marker
- family favorite = warmer, more iconic heirloom-style badge

A family favorite should feel like:
- “this dish matters to us”

## 16. Memorial and Grief-Sensitive Design

This part must be handled with great care.

### Memorial mode should feel
- respectful
- gentle
- quiet
- loving
- non-exploitative

### Memorial styling
Use:
- softer contrast
- lighter motion
- subtle badges
- dignified spacing
- restrained emotional design

Avoid:
- dramatic dark mourning interfaces
- manipulative sentimentality
- excessive animation
- uncanny voice or portrait realism

### Product principle
Grief support in the app should come from:
- preservation
- remembrance
- ritual
- warmth
- continuity

Not from emotional spectacle.

### Personalization layers
- family-level theme
- member-level cookbook style
- recipe-level cultural context
- memorial mode styling
- generation-aware visual tone

### What can be personalized
- cookbook cover style
- accent palette
- motif style
- ornament density
- typography flavor
- recipe card framing
